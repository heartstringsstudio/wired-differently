Chapter 23

Practical Worksheets

Tools You Can Actually Use

The worksheets in this chapter are designed to be used — repeatedly, imperfectly, and without the expectation that a single completed version will solve anything permanently. Print them if that works for you. Copy them into a notebook. Adapt them. The format matters far less than the honesty of the engagement with them. A worksheet that is partially completed and genuinely used is more valuable than a perfectly filled-out version that was completed once and filed away. Most of these worksheets are designed to be returned to periodically — weekly, monthly, or at significant transition points — rather than completed once definitively. The ADHD brain changes across conditions, seasons, and life circumstances. The worksheets that track it need to change with it. Where worksheets reference other chapters, those chapters contain the conceptual foundation for the questions. The worksheet is the application; the chapter is the understanding that makes the application meaningful. If yes: __________________________________________________________________________ A Note on Using These Worksheets These eight worksheets are practical tools, not performance tests. A worksheet that is used messily and incompletely, returned to inconsistently, and adapted freely to your actual needs is functioning correctly. A worksheet that is completed perfectly once and then avoided because the standard set the first time is too high to repeat is not. The ADHD brain benefits most from tools that are low-friction enough to use on difficult days, honest enough to reveal what is actually happening, and specific enough to point toward concrete actions rather than abstract intentions. These worksheets are designed to meet that standard. Use them in the service of building the actual life you're trying to build — not as additional items on the list of things you should be doing better.
